About Ashley

A junior at Northwestern University majoring in Journalism and minoring in Legal Studies and Spanish

I currently write for the North by Northwestern magazine's politics section, where I focus on local politiccs and national topics relating to immigration and whatever our President has gotten into recently. In the spring, I will complete my Journalism Residency at Pacific Standard magazine, an award-winning magazine that combines research with ambitious narrative and investigative reporting. Next fall, I'll continue my Medill education as a graduate student taking part in the Accelerated Master's Program. I look forward to learning more about my passion for reporting in the Investigative & Social Justice Reporting Track.

Last summer I worked at Injustice Watch, an investigative journalism publication focused on uncovering injustices in the criminal justice system. While there, I researched and interviewed to get to the bottom of systemic issues within Chicagoâ€™s Cook County criminal justice system. I gained an intimate knowledge of court cases and public records and how to recover them, as well as a familiarity with placing myself in the center of the action when I spent two weeks court watching at the Cook County criminal courthouse. I also delved into the dark world of detainee deaths in ICE detention centers, a story which uncovered unacknowledged neglect for undocumented immigrants.

I'm from Minnesota, but I love living in Evanston and exploring Chicago neighborhoods. In the fall of 2017 I lived in Bilbao, Spain where I studied Spanish and European politics. My time living abroad took me out of my comfort zone and opened me to new cultures -- my adventures inspired me to continue reporting in Spanish and writing about immigration.
