About Ashley

An investigative reporter based in Washington, D.C.

Ashley Hackett is an award-winning investigative reporter and double graduate of Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism. Her past work has focused on immigration, criminal justice, the foster care system, and environmental justice.

Ashley's work appears in many outlets, including CNN, Injustice Watch, Planet Forward, and North by Northwestern. She also won a Best of Medill Award for international reporting in Panama.

Her most recent internship at Pacific Standard led her to interview an Obama-era White House official about immigration, investigate jail suicides in San Diego, speak with a Harvard sociologist about the failures plaguing the U.S. criminal justice system, and report on the controversial 287(g) ICE immigration policy. She also enjoyed playing beach volleyball and exploring Southern California during her time at Pacific Standard's Santa Barbara office.

At Northwestern, Ashley enjoys giving prospective student tours of Medill as an Undergraduate Student Ambassador, writing for North by Northwestern magazine, running headlong into political debates, and paddle boarding in Lake Michigan (only in the summer). Though Minnesota born-and-raised, she's enjoyed living and reporting in Chicago, Santa Barbara, and Bilbao, Spain. She embraces any opportunity to learn from new cultures.
